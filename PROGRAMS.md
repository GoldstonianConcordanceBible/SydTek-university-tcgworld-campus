# SydTek University Programs

This document lists the current program architecture for SydTek University.

Programs should be built with truthful, structured language and should clearly distinguish between:

• pathway  
• certificate  
• advanced pathway  
• transcripted completion record  
• bachelor-style track  

Until formal legal authority exists, programs should not be described as accredited degrees.

---

## Program Families

### 1. Foundational Certificates
Short-to-mid-length structured pathways for foundational learning.

Typical characteristics:

• 20 to 60 playlist hours  
• 1 to 3 books  
• 4 to 10 written reflections  
• quiz, essay, or final synthesis  

Examples:
• Certificate in Gospel Foundations  
• Certificate in Canon Studies  
• Certificate in Biblical Philosophy  
• Certificate in the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine  

---

### 2. Advanced Certificates
Longer structured pathways for deeper study.

Typical characteristics:

• 60 to 120 playlist hours  
• 3 to 6 books  
• 8 to 16 written works  
• one or more integrative projects  

Examples:
• Advanced Certificate in Biblical Theology  
• Advanced Certificate in Canon Mapping  
• Advanced Certificate in GCB Interpretive Studies  

---

### 3. Comprehensive Study Tracks
Large multi-cluster pathways that function as bachelor-style academic structures.

Typical characteristics:

• 400 to 1200 playlist hours  
• 8 to 20 books  
• 20 to 40 written works  
• capstone or thesis component  

Examples:
• Bachelor-Style Pathway in Biblical Philosophy and Canon Studies  
• Advanced Independent Study Track in Theological Interpretation  
• Research Pathway in GCB Knowledge Architecture  

---

### 4. Research and Thesis Pathways
High-level demonstration pathways designed around synthesis and public scholarship.

Typical characteristics:

• advanced reading clusters  
• prior pathway completion preferred  
• thesis, portfolio, or public teaching demonstration  
• faculty or assessor review  

Examples:
• Senior Thesis Pathway  
• GCB Public Scholarship Pathway  
• SydTek Research Fellow Track

## Current Example Programs

### Certificate in Gospel Foundations

Purpose:
To introduce learners to the Gospels as a structured foundation for biblical understanding.

Requirements:
• 40 playlist hours  
• 2 books  
• 8 written reflections  
• final assessment  

Public Claim Level:
certificate_pathway

---

### Certificate in Canon Studies

Purpose:
To introduce learners to broader canon structure, including the 81-Book Ethiopian Canon context.

Requirements:
• 50 playlist hours  
• 2 books  
• canon-mapping project  
• final assessment  

Public Claim Level:
certificate_pathway

---

### Certificate in Biblical Philosophy

Purpose:
To develop foundational understanding of biblical philosophy through structured playlists, reading, and reflective synthesis.

Requirements:
• 60 playlist hours  
• 3 books  
• 10 written reflections  
• comparative essay  

Public Claim Level:
certificate_pathway

---

### Advanced Certificate in GCB Interpretive Studies

Purpose:
To train learners in the interpretive architecture of the Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB), especially through the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine.

Requirements:
• 90 playlist hours  
• 4 books  
• 12 written reflections  
• 2 integrative essays  
• final project  

Public Claim Level:
advanced_certificate_pathway

---

### Bachelor-Style Pathway in Biblical Philosophy and Canon Studies

Purpose:
To organize advanced study into a four-level, transcript-style pathway.

Requirements:
• 1200 playlist hours  
• 15 to 20 books  
• 30 to 40 written works  
• multiple projects  
• senior thesis  

Public Claim Level:
advanced_independent_study_track

---

## Program Approval Standard

No program should be considered active until it has:

• a documented title  
• a requirement structure  
• an assessment logic  
• a registry definition  
• a public claim level  
• a pathway owner or faculty guide  

---

## Summary

Programs are the structured academic containers of SydTek University.  
They must always be more than content bundles.  
They must be organized learning commitments.
