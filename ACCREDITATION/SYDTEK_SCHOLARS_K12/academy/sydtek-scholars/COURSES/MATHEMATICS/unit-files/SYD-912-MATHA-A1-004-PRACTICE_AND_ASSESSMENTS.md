# E) Practice Set 4 — Modeling with Inequalities (Word Problems)

Write an inequality, solve it, and interpret in words.

1) You must be at least 16 to drive. Let a = age.  
2) A movie ticket costs $12. You have $45. Let t = number of tickets.  
3) A phone plan charges $25 plus $5 per GB. You can spend at most $60. Let g = GB.  
4) You need more than 70 points to pass. Your score is s.  
5) You are saving for a $200 goal. You have $35 and save $15 per week. Let w = weeks.

### Answer Key (model + solution)
1) a ≥ 16  
2) 12t ≤ 45 ⇒ t ≤ 3.75 ⇒ t ≤ 3 tickets  
3) 25 + 5g ≤ 60 ⇒ 5g ≤ 35 ⇒ g ≤ 7  
4) s > 70  
5) 35 + 15w ≥ 200 ⇒ 15w ≥ 165 ⇒ w ≥ 11

**Format:** 2 solve + 1 graph/interpret.

### Exit Ticket Day 1
1) x − 4 ≥ 9  
2) 3y < 15  
3) Graph: x > -2  
**Key:** x ≥ 13; y < 5; open circle at -2, shade right

### Exit Ticket Day 2
1) -2x ≥ 10  
2) 5 + a ≤ 1  
3) Graph: a ≤ -3  
**Key:** x ≤ -5; a ≤ -4; closed circle -3 shade left

### Exit Ticket Day 3
1) 2x + 1 < 9  
2) 4 − y ≤ 2  
3) Does y = 1 satisfy 4 − y ≤ 2?  
**Key:** x < 4; -y ≤ -2 ⇒ y ≥ 2; No

### Exit Ticket Day 4
1) 3(y − 1) ≥ 12  
2) 7 − 4a > 15  
3) Graph solution to #2  
**Key:** y ≥ 5; -4a > 8 ⇒ a < -2; open circle -2 shade left

### Exit Ticket Day 5
1) 6x − 3 ≤ 21  
2) 2y + 8 > 3y + 1  
3) Interpret: y < 7 means what in words?  
**Key:** x ≤ 4; y < 7; y is less than 7

### Exit Ticket Day 6
1) Solve AND: -2 ≤ x < 3  
2) Solve OR: y ≤ 1 or y > 5  
3) Graph #2  
**Key:** [-2,3); (-∞,1] ∪ (5,∞)

### Exit Ticket Day 7
1) 2x + 5 ≥ 11 and x < 10  
2) Simplify: x ≥ 3 and x ≥ -1  
3) Graph #1  
**Key:** x ≥ 3 and x < 10 ⇒ [3,10); x ≥ 3

### Exit Ticket Day 8
1) Model: “no more than 8” using n  
2) Model: “at least -4” using x
