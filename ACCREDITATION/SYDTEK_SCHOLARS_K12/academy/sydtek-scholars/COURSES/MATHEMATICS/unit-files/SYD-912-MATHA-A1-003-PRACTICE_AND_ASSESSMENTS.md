# A) Daily Warm-Ups (8 days)

### Day 1
1) Solve: x + 5 > 12  
2) Solve: y − 7 ≤ 3  
3) Graph: x ≥ -2  
4) Graph: y < 4  
5) Write one number that satisfies: n > 10

### Day 2
1) Solve: 3x < 18  
2) Solve: -2y ≥ 8  
3) Solve: x/4 ≤ 6  
4) Graph: x ≤ 1  
5) Explain in one sentence: why does the sign flip sometimes?

### Day 3
1) Solve: 2x − 3 ≥ 7  
2) Solve: 5y + 4 < 19  
3) Solve: 4 − a ≤ 10  
4) Solve: 3m − 8 > 1  
5) Check: Does x = 6 satisfy 2x − 3 ≥ 7?

### Day 4
1) Solve: -3x > 9  
2) Solve: -5 + 2y ≤ 1  
3) Solve: 7 − 4a ≥ 15  
4) Solve: 2(3m − 1) < 10  
5) Graph your answer on a number line.

### Day 5
1) Solve: 6x − 5 ≤ 19  
2) Solve: 2y + 8 > 3y + 1  
3) Solve: 4a − 2 ≥ 2a + 6  
4) Solve: 7m + 3 < 5m + 11  
5) Identify: one solution or many solutions?

### Day 6
1) Solve AND: -2 ≤ x ≤ 5  
2) Solve AND: 3 < y ≤ 9  
3) Solve OR: x < -1 or x ≥ 4  
4) Write interval (if you know it): x ≥ 2  
5) Draw a number line for #3.

### Day 7
1) Solve: 2x + 1 ≥ 9 and x < 10  
2) Solve: y − 4 > 1 or y ≤ -2  
3) Solve: 3a − 2 ≤ 7 and 2a + 1 ≥ 5  
4) Graph your solution  
5) Pick a test value and verify it works.

### Day 8
1) Write an inequality for: “at least 12”  
2) Write an inequality for: “no more than 5”  
3) Model: “You have $20 and each item costs $3. How many items can you buy?”  
4) Solve your model  
5) Interpret your answer in words.

Solve and graph on a number line (open/closed circle):

1) x + 7 > 15  
2) y − 9 ≤ 4  
3) 6a < 42  
4) m/5 ≥ 3  
5) x − 3 ≥ -8  
6) 8y ≤ -24  
7) z/2 > -6  
8) a + 11 < 5  
9) 4m ≥ 28  
10) t/7 ≤ 2

1) x > 8  
2) y ≤ 13  
3) a < 7  
4) m ≥ 15  
5) x ≥ -5  
6) y ≤ -3  
7) z > -12  
8) a < -6
