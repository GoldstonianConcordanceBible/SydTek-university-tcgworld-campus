# AI in Education Policy (Students + Staff)

**Owner:** Academic Director  
**Review:** Quarterly

## 1. Allowed uses
- Drafting study guides and practice quizzes
- Explaining concepts with citations
- Language support and accessibility support
- Low-stakes formative feedback

## 2. Prohibited uses
- Undisclosed AI submission as original student work
- AI impersonation of teachers or authority figures
- Emotional manipulation, coercion, or “pressure” prompts
- Targeted persuasion for financial actions or token promotion
- High-stakes grading without human review pathway

## 3. Disclosure rules
If AI contributed materially to a submission, the student must:
- note tools used
- describe what AI did
- state what the student verified

## 4. Explainability
If AI influences grades, placement, discipline, or credentialing:
- student must receive an explanation
- student may request human review

## 5. Academic integrity
AI is a tool. Students remain responsible for:
- correctness
- citations
- originality
- compliance with assignment rules
