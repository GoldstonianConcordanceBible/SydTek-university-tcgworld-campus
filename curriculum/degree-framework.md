# SydTek University Degree Framework

This document provides a model for bachelor-style academic pathways within SydTek University.

These pathways are not to be publicly described as accredited degrees unless and until the institution gains the legal and recognized authority to make that claim.

For now, this framework should be understood as a structured advanced-study architecture.

---

## Degree-Style Pathway Model

Example pathway title:

Bachelor-Style Pathway in Biblical Philosophy and Canon Studies

This pathway is designed to organize a large body of study into a coherent, transcriptable structure.

The pathway combines:

• playlist-based instruction  
• companion books  
• reflective writing  
• projects  
• capstone synthesis  

---

## Framework Totals

Example full-program requirements:

• 1200 playlist watch hours  
• 15 to 20 books  
• 30 to 40 written reflections or essays  
• 8 to 12 projects or presentations  
• 1 capstone or senior thesis  

These totals may later be subdivided into academic levels and domains.

## Domain Clusters

A bachelor-style pathway should be organized by domain.

### Domain A: Canon Foundations
Examples:
• Introduction to the 81-Book Ethiopian Canon  
• Old Testament overview  
• New Testament overview  
• deuterocanonical and broader canon orientation  

### Domain B: Biblical Philosophy
Examples:
• covenant and kingdom  
• suffering and wisdom  
• law and justice  
• kingship and prophecy  
• judgment and restoration  

### Domain C: Theology and Doctrine
Examples:
• monotheism  
• angelology  
• demonology  
• salvation  
• ecclesiology  
• eschatology  

### Domain D: GCB Interpretive Layer
Examples:
• Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB) framework  
• Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine  
• canonical indexing  
• cross-textual synthesis  
• philosophical-theological interpretation  

### Domain E: Research and Demonstration
Examples:
• comparative essays  
• annotated bibliographies  
• teaching presentations  
• thesis writing  
• public-facing synthesis

## Reading Requirements

Books may come from:

• G. Rune Publishing  
• public-domain primary texts  
• approved companion texts  
• approved theological and philosophical works  

Reading requirements should always be tied to a clear pathway outcome.

A book should not be assigned only because it exists.  
It should serve the transformation of the learner.

---

## Assessment Distribution

A strong degree-style pathway should distribute assessment across the full program.

Example distribution:

• short reflections  
• interpretive essays  
• comparative essays  
• concept maps  
• oral or video presentations  
• quizzes  
• capstone project  
• senior thesis  

This ensures the learner is assessed through more than one mode.

---

## Thesis Model

The final thesis should function as a Fire-stage demonstration within the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine.

The thesis may include:

• a doctrinal analysis  
• a canon synthesis  
• a comparative theological study  
• a philosophical interpretation  
• an AI-readable metadata appendix  
• a teaching companion artifact  

This makes the final work both academically meaningful and structurally aligned with the GCB ecosystem.

---

## Transcript Fields

A degree-style pathway transcript may include:

• learner name  
• pathway title  
• course clusters completed  
• total playlist hours  
• total books completed  
• total written works completed  
• total projects completed  
• thesis title  
• completion date  
• assessor or faculty verification  

This creates serious documentation even before formal accreditation exists.

---

## Public Positioning

Externally, this framework should be described carefully as one of the following:

• bachelor-style pathway  
• advanced independent study pathway  
• structured four-level program  
• comprehensive certificate ladder  
• advanced transcripted completion track  

Until legal authority exists, public wording must remain disciplined.

---

## Summary

The SydTek degree framework transforms a large body of digital content into a coherent advanced-study architecture.

It is not merely a collection of playlists.  
It is a structured path from exposure to demonstration.

That is what makes it academically legible.
