# SydTek Scholars Homeschool Model

SydTek Scholars is the homeschool-facing learning pathway within the broader SydTek educational ecosystem.

This model is designed to support families who want a structured, documented, media-rich education pathway built around:

• playlist instruction  
• companion books  
• written narration and reflection  
• portfolio evidence  
• project-based learning  

The core principle is simple:

Learning is not only measured by seat time.  
Learning is demonstrated through exposure, reflection, retention, and application.

---

## Foundational Structure

For homeschool learners, the parent or guardian remains the primary educational supervisor until the learner reaches the age permitted by local law and jurisdiction.

The parent or guardian may choose:

• playlists  
• reading requirements  
• writing assignments  
• projects  
• pacing  
• assessment frequency  

This allows SydTek Scholars to function as a structured support system rather than a replacement for parental educational authority.

---

## Core Learning Formula

Each learning unit is built from four elements:

1. Watch  
2. Read  
3. Reflect  
4. Apply  

### Watch
Students view curated lecture playlists or instructional video sequences.

### Read
Students complete companion reading from approved texts, including G. Rune Publishing materials where assigned.

### Reflect
Students write age-appropriate responses, summaries, narrations, or essays.

### Apply
Students complete a project, presentation, oral recitation, worksheet, discussion, or portfolio artifact.

---

## Evidence Model

Families should maintain records showing:

• playlists assigned  
• playlists completed  
• books assigned  
• books completed  
• reflection responses  
• project submissions  
• dates of instruction  
• attendance or activity log  
• portfolio artifacts  

This creates a defensible paper trail for homeschool documentation.

---

## Example Weekly Flow

### Early Elementary
• 2 short playlist sessions  
• 1 reading block  
• 1 narration activity  
• 1 drawing or project artifact  

### Upper Elementary
• 3 playlist sessions  
• 2 reading blocks  
• 1 written reflection  
• 1 quiz or discussion  

### Middle School
• 3 to 5 playlist sessions  
• assigned chapters  
• 1 short essay  
• 1 project or presentation  

### High School
• structured watch-hour targets  
• companion text reading  
• written essays  
• discussion prompts  
• portfolio assessment

## Parent Role

Parents function as:

• selector of pathway  
• overseer of pacing  
• verifier of completion  
• custodian of records  
• evaluator or co-evaluator of written work  

This is especially important because laws vary by jurisdiction.

## Jurisdictional Principle

SydTek Scholars does not claim that one model automatically satisfies every legal requirement in every jurisdiction.

Instead, this framework is designed to help families create:

• documented instruction  
• trackable progress  
• portfolio-ready evidence  
• structured educational continuity  

Families remain responsible for aligning implementation with local legal requirements.
