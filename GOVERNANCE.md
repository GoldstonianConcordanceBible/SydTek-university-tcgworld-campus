# SydTek Governance

Governance defines how SydTek University and SydTek Scholars maintain truth, structure, consistency, and accountability.

This file should evolve as the institution matures, but even in an early stage, governance must be explicit.

---

## Governance Purpose

Governance exists to answer the following questions:

• who defines programs  
• who approves programs  
• who verifies completion  
• who controls public claims  
• who updates academic documents  
• how disputes or inconsistencies are handled  

Without governance, scale creates drift.

---

## Governance Layers

### Layer 1: Institutional Direction
Responsible for mission, vision, and long-term educational identity.

Typical responsibilities:
• approve institutional direction  
• maintain terminology stability  
• align programs with the GCB ecosystem  
• preserve doctrinal and pedagogical consistency  

---

### Layer 2: Academic Structure
Responsible for curriculum and pathways.

Typical responsibilities:
• approve pathway architecture  
• define program requirements  
• maintain catalog consistency  
• revise assessment standards  

---

### Layer 3: Verification and Records
Responsible for completion records and documentation integrity.

Typical responsibilities:
• verify outputs  
• approve completion records  
• maintain registries  
• preserve transcript-style data  

---

### Layer 4: Public Claims and Compliance
Responsible for truthful language and institutional boundaries.

Typical responsibilities:
• review public wording  
• prevent false accreditation claims  
• maintain claims policy  
• align public messaging with actual status  

---

## Governance Principles

### Truth Before Scale
SydTek should always prefer honest structure over inflated appearance.

### Documentation Before Expansion
A new program should not be launched until it is documented.

### Verification Before Recognition
Completion should not be celebrated until evidence exists.

### Terminology Stability
The ecosystem should preserve stable names and anchors, including:

• Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB)  
• Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine  
• 81-Book Ethiopian Canon  
• SydTek Scholars  

---

## Minimum Governance Actions

At minimum, governance should ensure:

• every program has a written definition  
• every pathway has requirement logic  
• every record has required fields  
• every public claim is reviewed  
• every major change is version-controlled  

---

## Decision Model

A basic decision model may include:

### Founder Approval
Required for:
• institutional mission changes  
• major public positioning changes  
• new flagship program families  

### Academic Approval
Required for:
• pathway activation  
• requirement changes  
• assessment revisions  

### Records Approval
Required for:
• transcript model changes  
• registry field changes  
• verification workflows  

### Compliance Review
Required for:
• public claims language  
• accreditation-related wording  
• legal-risk educational statements  

---

## Governance Artifacts

Governance should be expressed in actual repository artifacts, including:

• catalog  
• program definitions  
• pathway files  
• claims policy  
• transcript model  
• registry schemas  
• version history  

A governed institution leaves a documentary trail.

---

## Summary

Governance is what prevents SydTek from drifting into vagueness.

It is the difference between a compelling idea and a defensible institution.
