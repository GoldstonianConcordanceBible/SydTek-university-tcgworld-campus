# Concordance Spine Anchor (GCB) — SydTek Campus Edition
**Purpose:** Provide a standardized, repeatable structure for study entries, exhibits, quests, and AI-agent teaching outputs.  
**Method:** Mirror → Water → Fire (M/W/F) is embedded in each section.

---

- **Anchor(s):**
- **Scope Boundary (1–2 lines):** What this entry does NOT attempt to prove.

---

## 1) Purpose Thesis (M/W/F)
**Mirror (Evidence):** What is directly observed in the anchor(s)?  
**Water (Synthesis):** What is the coherent theme the anchor(s) support?  
**Fire (Practice):** What is the intended transformation or outcome (without guarantees)?

**Output (2–5 sentences):**  
- Purpose thesis should be short, testable, and non-performative.

---

## 2) Canon Notes (Mirror-heavy)
**Goal:** Preserve what can be pointed to without overreach.

- Definitions (key terms)
- Context notes (time, place, speaker, audience where applicable)
- Language notes (if relevant; avoid pretending expertise)
- Boundaries (“We cannot conclude X from this alone.”)

**Output:** Bulleted, 6–15 bullets total.

---

## 3) Narrative Flow (Water-heavy)
**Goal:** Show progression and internal logic.

- What happens before/after?
- What is the thread of reasoning?
- What is the pivot point?

**Output:** 1 paragraph + 3 bullets.

---

## 4) Core Theology (Mirror → Water)
**Goal:** Identify doctrinal claims that are actually supported.

**Mirror:** What claims are directly grounded in anchor(s)?  
**Water:** How do cross-links support/limit these claims?

**Output:**  
- 3–7 claims, each labeled:
  - **Grounded** (direct)
  - **Supported** (cross-link dependent)
  - **Interpretive** (clearly labeled as interpretation)

---

## 5) Messiah Thread (If applicable)
**Goal:** Track messianic themes responsibly (no forced links).

**Mirror:** Identify explicit references (if any).  
**Water:** Identify legitimate typology or thematic echoes (with reasons).  
**Fire:** Describe how this informs worship/practice without weaponizing.

**Output:** 1 short section; “Not applicable” is allowed.

---

## 6) Cross-Links (Water discipline)
**Rule:** Every cross-link must include: “why it connects” + “what it adds.”

### Cross-Link Entry Template
- Cross-link:
- Why it connects:
- What it adds:
- Tension introduced (if any):

**Minimum:** 3 cross-links.

---

## 7) Hard Questions (Water honesty)
**Goal:** Mark uncertainty and protect against fake certainty.

Hard questions must be:
- honest
- specific
- not performative
- not “solved” unless truly solved

**Minimum:** 1 hard question.

---

## 8) Preacher’s Toolkit (Fire-ready, not hype)
**Goal:** Provide teaching tools that are disciplined and safe.

Include:
- 3 talking points (grounded)
- 1 caution (what not to claim)
- 1 illustration idea (optional)
- 1 discussion question

**Guardrail:** No “guaranteed outcomes.”

---

## 9) Walk in the Word (Fire practice plan)
**Goal:** Turn theology into a small, repeatable action.

Include:
- Practice step (specific)
- Time window (today/this week)
- Accountability mechanism
- One measurable indicator

**Guardrail:** Practice is obedience-oriented, not outcome-oriented.

---

## 10) Prayer + Practice (Fire + humility)
**Goal:** Close with humility and alignment.

- Prayer prompt (1–3 sentences)
- Practice commitment (1 sentence)
- Guardrail statement (1 sentence)

Example guardrail:
> I will not use this interpretation to coerce, shame, or promise outcomes to others.
